# Job Assessment Tool — Concept

## What it is

The Job Assessment Tool is an AI-powered career risk analyzer designed for high school students choosing what to study. Students enter any occupation — and optionally a company — and receive a transparent 0-100 score showing how exposed that role is to AI displacement, alongside an eight-dimension breakdown, real cited evidence, recent news developments, and a list of more-resilient adjacent careers.

## Why it matters

Career guidance today still says "follow your passion" — but no one is honestly telling students which passions are walking into a wall. An aspiring paralegal, accountant, or graphic designer deserves to know exactly how AI is reshaping their target field, so they can either steer toward higher ground or build the right skills to stay relevant.

## How it works

Every analysis is grounded in real, verifiable data: the official US Department of Labor O*NET task list for the occupation, SEC EDGAR filings when a company is specified, and a curated news feed pulled live from 17 trusted sources (BBC, NYT, Bloomberg, Financial Times, MIT Technology Review, IEEE Spectrum, and others — categorized by trust tier). The AI synthesizes this evidence into a structured analysis, but every claim shows its source. Students don't have to trust the tool — they can trace the reasoning.

## My role vs. AI's role

I designed the eight-dimension scoring framework, calibrated the score anchors against known reference occupations, curated the trusted-source policy, designed the constellation visual entry experience, and built the discovery quiz for students who don't know where to start. AI is a feature *of* the tool — it analyzes data — and AI was a coding assistant. Every substantive design and content decision is mine.

## Target user

A Grade 11 student deciding what to study. They don't necessarily know what AI displacement means. They want an honest read on the future they're walking into, presented in a way that respects their intelligence and gives them a path forward.
