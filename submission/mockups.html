Job Assessment Tool · user journey

An AI-powered career risk analyzer for high school students. Three core surfaces — Discovery, Analysis, Verification — work together to give students an honest, sourced view of how AI is reshaping the careers they're considering.

01 / DISCOVERY
Find your future

A galactic map of every industry surrounds the central hub. Students search any occupation, take a 30-second matching quiz to surface careers that fit their interests, or click any node to drill in. Designed to make exploration feel inviting rather than intimidating.

Design choices
  • Discovery over search — instead of a blank input box, students see a constellation of every industry. Reduces the "I don't know what to type" barrier.
  • Color-coded by industry — eight categories, each a distinct hue. Students intuitively grasp the breadth of options.
  • Quiz CTA pulses gently — for students who don't know where to start, four questions surface careers that match how they describe themselves.
02 / ANALYSIS
The honest verdict

Once a student picks an occupation (and optional company), they get a transparent 0-100 risk score with confidence band, an eight-dimension breakdown, evidence ledger, model reasoning trail, and adjacent more-resilient careers. Every claim shows its source.

Design choices
  • The score is the centerpiece, but never floats alone — every analysis comes with a confidence band, a verdict in plain language, and visible context.
  • Eight dimensions, not one — instead of "75% at risk," students see why: which specific traits of the job make it more or less exposed.
  • Industry baseline + company adjustment — students see both the general industry score and how the specific company shifts it (e.g., Software Developer at Apple is 8 points safer than the industry average).
  • Always cited — sources include O*NET (US Dept of Labor), SEC filings, and recent news from trusted outlets.

Design choices
  • Trust tiers, not just sources — every source is labeled by editorial standard. Students can see the difference between a news wire and a company press release.
  • 17 verified outlets only — TechCrunch, VentureBeat, Hacker News, opinion blogs, and substacks are explicitly excluded.
  • Editorial layout, not feed-of-headlines — large typography, italic pulled quotes, real "why this matters" summaries. Designed to be read, not scrolled past.
