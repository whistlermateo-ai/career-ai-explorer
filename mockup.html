How the score was reached

1
Identified the occupation
Mapped "Software Developer" to SOC 15-1252 via O*NET. Confidence in mapping: high. Pulled the official 22-task definition.
2
Scored each task individually
For each of 22 tasks, classified as routine-language (14), human-judgment (5), coordination (3). Cross-referenced against current frontier model capabilities. Routine-language tasks heavily exposed; coordination tasks largely not.
3
Weighted by time-share
O*NET work-activity data shows the average software developer spends roughly 45% of work hours on language tasks, 30% on judgment tasks, 25% on coordination. Weighted accordingly.
4
Cross-referenced with research datasets
Felten exposure: 0.71 → maps to ~67. McKinsey automation potential: 63% → maps to ~63. Anthropic Economic Index ranking: top 5% → contributes upward pressure. Three datasets converge in the 60–70 range. Industry baseline locked at 60.
5
Applied company-specific adjustments
Apple-specific factors: zero engineering layoffs over 24 months −4, hardware-software integration moat −3, heavy AI investment that augments rather than replaces engineers per leadership −2, but entry-level hiring decline of 18% YoY +1. Net adjustment: −8. Final score: 52.
6
Set confidence band
All three primary datasets agreed within ±5 points. Company-specific signals are recent and well-documented. Confidence: high (52 ± 6). Largest source of uncertainty: pace of model capability improvement over the next 24 months.

What you can do in Grade 11

01
Take a high-level math course (AP Calc BC or IB HL Math)
ML and systems work both rest on linear algebra and calculus. Doing this in Grade 11 unlocks the harder uni courses early.
02
Build something real, end-to-end, using an AI API
Not a tutorial clone. A small original tool you ship publicly. Demonstrates the meta-skill of working with AI rather than against it.
03
Learn one platform-specific stack deeply
Swift + iOS, or Metal, or embedded C. Tacit platform knowledge is the part of "developer" hardest for AI to fully replicate, and the part Apple values most.
04
Practice writing and verbal communication
Engineers who write a clear design doc and present it to a room get promoted regardless of automation. Join debate, write a blog, run a club.
05
Apply to a structured summer program in ML or robotics
Inspirit AI, MIT BWSI, or a university research program. Earlier exposure to the resilient sub-fields matters.
